Advertising Currently: Trends and Challenges

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026

The advertising industry stands at a crossroads. In boardrooms from Sandton to Sydney, executives are grappling with a fundamental question: what does advertising mean in an age of algorithmic curation, privacy-first browsing, and AI-generated content?

This isn't just a technical question—it's an existential one. The traditional models that have defined our industry for decades are being disrupted simultaneously from multiple directions.

The Fragmentation Challenge

Remember when reaching your audience meant buying space on three television networks and two major newspapers? Those days are gone forever. Today's consumers scatter across dozens of platforms, each with its own culture, algorithms, and expectations.

The average South African internet user now spends 9+ hours online daily across multiple devices and platforms—up from just 4 hours in 2015.

This fragmentation creates enormous challenges for advertisers. How do you maintain a coherent brand message when your audience is simultaneously scrolling TikTok, checking Instagram, reading news on News24, browsing Takealot, and watching Showmax?

The Privacy Revolution

The third-party cookie is dying. Safari and Firefox already block them by default. Google Chrome will follow in late 2026. This isn't speculation—it's the biggest shift in digital advertising since the advent of the smartphone.

For South African advertisers, this creates a particular challenge. We rely heavily on programmatic advertising—over 60% of digital spend flows through automated bidding systems. When cookies disappear, those systems lose their ability to track users across sites.

  • Contextual Targeting: Advertisers are returning to content-based targeting—placing ads based on what users are reading, not who they are
  • First-Party Data: Brands are investing heavily in building direct relationships with customers
  • Consent-Based Tracking: New solutions like Google's Privacy Sandbox aim to balance privacy with relevance
  • AI-Powered Solutions: Machine learning models that can target effectively without individual tracking

The agencies that will thrive are those that help clients build sustainable first-party data strategies—turning every customer interaction into an opportunity to learn and personalize.

The Rise of Short-Form Video

TikTok has fundamentally changed what audiences expect from advertising. The average view duration on short-form video is now under 3 seconds—advertisers have less time than ever to capture attention.

But here's the paradox: while attention spans shrink, engagement metrics for short-form content continue to soar. South Africans now spend an average of 2.5 hours daily on TikTok and Instagram Reells—up 180% since 2023.

For brands, this means embracing authenticity over polish. The most successful campaigns aren't the most expensive productions—they're the ones that feel real, immediate, and culturally relevant.

The Economic Pressures

South African advertisers face headwinds that their counterparts in developed markets don't experience. Load-shedding costs the economy billions annually. Currency volatility makes long-term planning difficult. Consumer confidence remains fragile.

Yet within these constraints, innovation thrives. South African agencies have become experts at doing more with less—creating impact with limited budgets, finding creative solutions to infrastructure challenges, and building resilient strategies that can adapt to rapid change.

Moving Forward

The challenges facing advertising are real—but so are the opportunities. The agencies that will thrive are those that embrace change, invest in new capabilities, and never lose sight of the fundamental truth: advertising is about connecting with people.

Technology will continue to evolve. Platforms will rise and fall. But the need for compelling stories, meaningful connections, and strategic thinking will never go away.
