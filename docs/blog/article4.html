Creativity in Advertising Context

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026

What makes an advertisement creative? Is it the visual polish, the clever wordplay, or the shock value? In today's hyper-saturated media environment, the definition of creativity is shifting from "how it looks" to "how it connects."

Creativity without context is just art. In advertising, creativity must be purposeful. It must solve a problem, change a perception, or drive an action. But as our attention spans shrink to the length of a TikTok scroll, the "context" part of that equation has become more important than ever.

The Contextual Shift

In the past, creative teams designed for "media buckets": the 30-second TV spot, the billboard, the full-page print ad. Today, we design for moments. We design for the "skip" button, the "mute" button, and the infinite scroll.

Creativity now means understanding the specific emotional state of a user on a specific platform at a specific time. An ad that works on LinkedIn will fail on TikTok, not because the message is wrong, but because the context is different.

Purpose-Driven Creativity

South African consumers, in particular, are increasingly looking for brands that stand for something. Creativity today is about finding authentic ways to tell those stories. It's about empathy—the ability to see the world through the consumer's eyes and create something that truly matters to them.
