Vibe Coding: Context-Specific Applications in Advertising, Sales & Service Quality

By Evans Mathibe Feb 24, 2026 15 min read

Let me share something I've been thinking about for a while now. The term "vibe coding" has been floating around the tech world—it refers to using AI to write code by describing what you want in natural language, essentially "coding by feel" rather than formal programming. But here's what fascinates me: this concept has applications far beyond software development. It can transform how we approach advertising, sales, and service delivery. Let me explain.

What Vibe Coding Actually Means

At its core, vibe coding is about leveraging AI to bridge the gap between intent and implementation. Instead of learning complex programming languages, you describe what you want in plain language, and AI generates the code. It's intuitive, fast, and democratizes technology creation.

But the underlying principle—using AI to translate human intent into action—is applicable anywhere we currently need specialized skills to get things done. That's the real transformation happening.

"Vibe coding isn't about replacing developers—it's about giving everyone the power to create. The same principle applies to marketing, sales, and service."

Application 1: Advertising

How Vibe Coding Applies to Advertising

Traditional advertising required teams of specialists: copywriters, designers, media buyers, analysts. Each role required years of training. Vibe coding principles are transforming this by allowing non-specialists to achieve professional results.

Campaign Creation

Describe your target audience and campaign goals in plain language. AI generates ad copy, suggests visuals, and identifies optimal channels.

A/B Testing

Instead of manually coding landing pages, describe variations you want to test. AI generates the code and sets up the testing framework.

Performance Analysis

Ask AI to analyze campaign data and suggest optimizations in conversational language rather than navigating complex analytics dashboards.

What This Means for South African Advertisers

  • Smaller agencies can now compete with larger ones on capability
  • Campaigns can be launched faster without technical bottlenecks
  • More iterations mean better optimization
  • Local businesses can access tools previously only available to big brands

Application 2: Sales

Vibe Coding in Sales Operations

Sales has always been about relationships and human connection. Vibe coding doesn't change that—it removes the administrative friction that takes salespeople away from what they do best.

Proposal Generation

Describe a client's needs conversationally. AI generates customized proposals with pricing, timelines, and scopes.

Follow-up Automation

Describe your follow-up sequence. AI builds the automation workflow without requiring technical setup.

CRM Management

Instead of manually updating records, describe interactions conversationally and let AI update your CRM.

For South African Sales Teams

  • Salespeople spend less time on admin and more time selling
  • Onboarding new sales staff becomes faster
  • Consistency in client communication improves
  • Small teams can operate like much larger ones

Application 3: Service Quality

Enhancing Service Delivery

Service quality in South Africa varies enormously—from world-class to concerning. Vibe coding principles can help businesses consistently deliver better experiences without requiring massive investments in systems and training.

Response Templates

Describe common customer scenarios. AI generates appropriate response templates that maintain your brand voice.

Knowledge Bases

Instead of manually documenting everything, describe your products/services conversationally. AI builds searchable knowledge bases.

Feedback Analysis

Describe what feedback insights you need. AI analyzes reviews, surveys, and social mentions to identify patterns.

Improving Service in Our Context

  • Language barriers can be addressed with AI translation while maintaining local nuance
  • Consistent service across multiple locations becomes achievable
  • Small businesses can offer support previously only possible for enterprises

The Common Thread

What unites all these applications is the same principle: removing technical barriers so people can focus on what matters—creativity, relationships, and delivering value. In each case, AI handles the implementation while humans provide the vision and judgment.

Getting Started

If you're looking to apply vibe coding principles in your business, here's my advice:

  • Start small: Identify one repetitive task that takes time but doesn't require complex judgment
  • Describe, don't prescribe: Tell AI what you want to achieve, not exactly how to do it
  • Review and refine: AI generates first drafts; humans provide the polish
  • Build on success: Once you see results, expand to other areas

The Bottom Line

Vibe coding isn't just a tech trend—it's a shift in how we think about capability. The businesses that thrive in this new environment will be those that learn to articulate what they want clearly and then judge whether AI's output meets their standards. Technical skills are being replaced by prompt skills and evaluation skills.

In South Africa, where resources are often constrained and creativity is abundant, this shift represents a genuine opportunity. We can now achieve more with less, compete with larger players, and focus our uniquely human talents on what truly matters: understanding our customers and creating value for them.
