How AI is Affecting the Entire Industry in South Africa and the World

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026 • Page 1 of 5

In the neon-lit streets of Sandton, where banking towers pierce the Johannesburg skyline, a quiet revolution unfolds. It's not visible in protest marches or parliament debates—it's happening in the algorithms that determine which advertisement you'll see on your phone, which brand will catch your eye, and which message will finally make you click "buy."

Artificial intelligence has arrived in South Africa's advertising industry, and its impact is nothing short of seismic. From the creative agencies of Rosebank to the media buying desks of Cape Town, AI is reshaping every facet of how we conceive, create, and deliver marketing messages.

The Current Landscape

South Africa's advertising industry contributes approximately R45 billion annually to the economy, employing over 30,000 professionals across creative agencies, media shops, and digital consultancies. But here's the uncomfortable truth: many of these roles are now facing fundamental disruption.

"AI isn't going to replace advertising professionals—but advertising professionals who use AI will replace those who don't." — Andrej Karpathy, former Tesla AI Director

The numbers tell a stark story. According to the Interactive Advertising Bureau South Africa (IAB SA), 78% of local agencies have already integrated some form of AI into their operations, up from just 23% in 2022. The acceleration is breathtaking.

But it's not just about adoption rates. It's about the fundamental restructuring of creative work itself. In agencies across Johannesburg, Durban, and Pretoria, copywriters now collaborate with AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude. Designers use Midjourney and DALL-E to rapidly prototype concepts. Media buyers rely on algorithmic bidding systems that optimize spend in real-time.
