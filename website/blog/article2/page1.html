Advertising Currently: Trends and Challenges

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026 • Page 1 of 5

The advertising industry stands at a crossroads. In boardrooms from Sandton to Sydney, executives are grappling with a fundamental question: what does advertising mean in an age of algorithmic curation, privacy-first browsing, and AI-generated content?

This isn't just a technical question—it's an existential one. The traditional models that have defined our industry for decades are being disrupted simultaneously from multiple directions.

The Fragmentation Challenge

Remember when reaching your audience meant buying space on three television networks and two major newspapers? Those days are gone forever. Today's consumers scatter across dozens of platforms, each with its own culture, algorithms, and expectations.

The average South African internet user now spends 9+ hours online daily across multiple devices and platforms—up from just 4 hours in 2015.

This fragmentation creates enormous challenges for advertisers. How do you maintain a coherent brand message when your audience is simultaneously scrolling TikTok, checking Instagram, reading news on News24, browsing Takealot, and watching Showmax?
