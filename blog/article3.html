Is It Still Worth Going to School for Advertising?

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026

It's a question being asked in dorm rooms and creative departments across the country: in an age where you can learn Midjourney in an afternoon and master TikTok trends by scrolling for a week, is a three-year advertising degree still relevant?

The traditional path into our industry—the expensive portfolio school or the specialized marketing degree—is facing its own disruption. As the tools of production become democratized and the platforms evolve faster than any curriculum can keep up with, the value proposition of formal education is changing.

The Curriculum Lag

The primary challenge for traditional institutions is speed. It can take years for a new course to be approved and integrated into a university curriculum. In that same time, AI has gone from a curiosity to the central engine of the industry.

Many students find themselves learning theories from the 1990s and tools from the 2010s while the industry has already moved into the 2026 reality of real-time, AI-augmented campaign delivery.

What Schools Still Do Best

Despite the challenges, formal education still offers something that YouTube tutorials cannot: foundational thinking. A good advertising school doesn't just teach you how to use Photoshop; it teaches you how to think, how to solve problems, and how to understand human psychology.

  • Strategic Foundation: Understanding the "why" behind a campaign, not just the "how"
  • Critical Thinking: The ability to dissect a brief and find the hidden insight
  • Collaboration: Learning to work in diverse teams under pressure
  • Network: Building relationships with peers who will be your future colleagues and clients

The Hybrid Future

The most successful young professionals entering the industry today aren't choosing between formal education and self-teaching—they're doing both. They use the degree to build their foundational thinking and online platforms to master the latest technical skills.
