How AI is Affecting the Entire Industry in South Africa and the World

By Evans Mathibe • February 2026 • Page 3 of 5

Creative Destruction or Creative Augmentation?

The doomsayers have had their say. They've predicted mass unemployment in advertising, the death of creativity, and the homogenization of all marketing messages into an AI-generated sludge. But the reality on the ground tells a different story.

78%

of South African agencies have integrated AI into their operations (IAB SA, 2025)

What we're seeing is not the replacement of human creativity, but its amplification. The most successful agencies in South Africa—those winning awards at the Loeries, those securing global accounts—are those that have figured out how to use AI as a force multiplier for human imagination.

"The best creative work still comes from human intuition, emotion, and cultural understanding. But AI helps us get there faster, test more ideas, and refine our craft." — Peter Steinberger, renowned creative director

Consider the workflow of a modern creative team in Johannesburg. Instead of spending hours on initial concepts, they use AI to generate hundreds of rapid prototypes. The human team then selects, refines, and elevates the best ideas—spending their energy on the work that actually requires human judgment: understanding the client's business, connecting with their audience, and crafting messages that resonate on an emotional level.

This shift has profound implications for talent. The skills that matter most are no longer just creative execution—they're creative direction, strategic thinking, and the ability to guide AI tools toward meaningful outputs.
